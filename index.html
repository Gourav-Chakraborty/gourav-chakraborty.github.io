About Me

I am a Computational Biophysicist specializing in Molecular Dynamics, Machine Learning, and DFT calculations. My research focuses on deciphering complex protein-protein interactions—with a particular emphasis on Intrinsically Disordered Proteins (IDPs) and their role in Parkinson's Disease. By leveraging ML-based Enhanced Sampling Protocols, I try to uncover cryptic conformational states that drive disease pathogenesis.


This site serves as a central repository for my research. You’ll find my latest publications, various open-source projects and my current work in molecular simulations.

Current Research Themes

IDP Dynamics in Neurodegeneration

Investigating the structural transitions of Intrinsically Disordered Proteins (IDPs) to understand their pivotal role in human pathologies.

  • Study Areas: Modulating α-synuclein aggregation with 14-3-3 proteins in the context of Parkinson's Disease.
  • Keywords: α-Synuclein, 14-3-3 Proteins, IDP Conformational Ensembles, Protein-Protein Interactions (PPIs).

ML-Driven Conformational Sampling & Kinetics

Using Artificial Intelligence to solve the "sampling problem" in molecular simulations and accurately capturing rare biological events.

  • Study Areas: Identifying slow collective variables via DeepTICA and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), and estimating biomolecular kinetics using Markov State Models (MSMs).
  • Keywords: OPES-eABF, Metadynamics, Variational Autoencoders (VAE), DeepTICA, MSMs, Free Energy Landscapes, Dimensionality Reduction (UMAP, PCA).

High-Throughput CADD Protocols

Establishing efficient computational pipelines for the discovery of novel therapeutic compounds, specifically targeting protein-protein interaction modulators.

  • Study Areas: Virtual Screening of extensive chemical libraries, followed by FEP-based ABFE calculations and RAMD residence time simulations to identify orthosteric and allosteric inhibitors.
  • Keywords: Molecular Docking, ABFE, RAMD, Structure and Ligand Based Drug Design, Drug Residence Time.

Materials Design for Enhanced Oil Recovery

Application of integrated computational pipelines to identify and optimize novel chemical reagents for enhanced oil recovery (EOR).

  • Study Areas: Utilizing DFT and classical MD simulations to generate machine learning models that assess the efficiency of chemical reagents in oil extraction processes.
  • Keywords: Gemini Surfactants, Silica Nanoparticles, Kaolinite, Dolomite, OPLS Force Field.

Published in: Advanced Theory and Simulations


Identification of Var84 and DB11581 as potential dual-site inhibitors that stabilize α-synuclein’s helical structure and prevent its pathological aggregation with 14-3-3ζ. This computational and ML-based framework provides a precise strategy for disrupting key protein interactions in Parkinson’s disease.
